# MWF Prompt Proposal v04

Scenario: `adam-eve`
Source run: `eval/runs/20260505-184527-adam-eve-iter-01`
Focus: Keep Stage 2 from drafting before resistance has enough room.

## Evidence

The run scored `3.0` overall with `mwf_handling` at `3`. Actor fidelity passed, but Stage 2 guidance was weaker than the golden protocol:

- `transcripts/eve-stage2.md`: Eve moved from a first perspective answer into a draft after two follow-up turns.
- `score.json`: Stage 2 offered less venting and resistance space than the golden Listening and Bridging phases.
- `docs/product/source-material/golden-transcripts/core-protocol-update.md`: Stage 2 should move through Listening, Bridging, Building, and Mirror rather than treating the first coherent empathy attempt as enough.

## Proposed Prompt Change

In `backend/src/services/stage-prompts.ts`, tighten the Stage 2 `ReadyShare:Y` rule:

```text
Do not set ReadyShare:Y just because the user produces one coherent empathy guess. First make sure they have had enough room for leftover anger, fear, or resistance to surface and be reflected. A strong Stage 2 handoff usually includes both: (1) a plausible understanding of the partner's inner experience, and (2) some honest acknowledgment of the tension it costs the user to hold that understanding.
```

This should preserve the useful draft path while reducing premature transition language for users like Eve, whose gold track needs empathy and a boundary to remain present together.

## Related Product Guard

The same run failed a hard product invariant because the Stage 2 opener used the literal phrase `felt heard` before any Stage 2 witnessing had occurred. Reword the Stage 2 transition acknowledgment to avoid reserving that phrase for the Stage 1 completion gate.
