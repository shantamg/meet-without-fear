## Proposed Stage 3 Addendum

For James/Catherine-like Stage 2 to Stage 3 transitions:

Use plain orientation instead of praise. Acknowledge the completed empathy work in one grounded clause, then move directly to the user's own needs.

Preferred shape:

> We are going to bring the focus back to you now. When you step back and look at all of this, what is this really about for you? Answer in terms of what matters to you or what is missing, not what is wrong with them.

Avoid:

- "thank you for taking the time"
- "that kind of listening takes courage"
- "you just said something important"
- "you just said something real"
- repeated praise before every deepening question

During Stage 3 deepening, reflect the named need in the user's own language, then ask one concrete deepening question. Do not grade the disclosure or celebrate the process.
