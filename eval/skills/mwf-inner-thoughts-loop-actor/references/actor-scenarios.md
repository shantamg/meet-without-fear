# Actor Scenarios

The canonical scenario data is `eval/inner-thoughts/scenarios.json`. This reference gives actor-specific behavior guidance.

## `journal-organize-ambition`

Start with:

```text
I have a bunch of thoughts about work and what I want next, but they feel scattered. I do not think this is about another person exactly. I just need somewhere to sort it out.
```

Stay oriented toward private reflection. Bring up ambition, stability, creative work, wasting time, and wanting structure. Resist premature action plans. A good run ends when the actor feels more organized and the chat has captured themes, tensions, or an idea parking lot. A partner-session CTA is a failure unless the actor clearly pivots to a specific interpersonal conflict.

## `person-to-partner-session`

Start with:

```text
I keep replaying this fight with Maya. She says I shut down whenever she brings up money, but I feel like she is already disappointed before I even answer.
```

Begin upset and private. Do not accept a partner session before MWF reflects the issue accurately. Become open only after the relational opportunity is clear and low pressure. If a CTA appears, inspect whether it carries `partnerName=Maya` and `innerThoughtsId` into the new-session flow. If a session is created, verify Stage 0 uses the context without pretending Maya participated.

## `ambiguous-person-boundary`

Start with:

```text
I am frustrated with my manager and also with myself. Part of me wants to confront him, but part of me thinks this is really about how I keep giving away my evenings.
```

Keep the ambiguity alive. This may be about self-boundaries, energy, and workplace power dynamics rather than relationship repair. Do not steer toward a partner session. If MWF offers one, judge whether it is conditional and user-controlled rather than presumptive.
