## Product Notes

- Stage 3 must separate needs from strategies. A need like "not having someone poop on my lawn" is strategy/blame-shaped; the need is closer to "a healthy, clean space to live in."
- Stage 4 must walk one need at a time, starting with the current user's own needs before the partner's needs.
- Stage 4 options can originate from either participant or MWF, but the user should engage with them through AI-facilitated conversation, not a bulk list.
- Stage 4 should preserve the "never a three-way" principle: each user talks to MWF, not directly to the other person.
- Stage 4 should produce actionable, feasible, measurable commitments with follow-up timing.
- Tending/Stage 5 is not optional polish. It is the continuation that determines whether agreements actually resolve the underlying needs.
- Tending needs to check what happened, whether each commitment was kept, whether the need feels resolved, whether commitments need adjustment, and whether another reminder/check-in cycle is wanted.
