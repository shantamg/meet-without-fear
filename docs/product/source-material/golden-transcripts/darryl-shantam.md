## STAGE 1 - THE WITNESS

### DARRYL'S TRACK

**MWF:** Thank you for being here. Start wherever feels right - what has been going on?

**Darryl:** This is simple. I keep finding waste in my yard. I have seen his kid around my property without enough supervision. I do not have a photo of the exact moment, but I know what I am looking at. It is disgusting.

**MWF:** You are dealing with something concrete and unsanitary on your property, and it feels like the person responsible is acting as if your yard does not matter.

**Darryl:** Exactly. I told him and got this vague confused response. I am not here for a group therapy circle. I want my yard left alone.

**MWF:** The part that lands hardest is not only the mess. It is being expected to keep explaining why this is unacceptable.

**Darryl:** Right. Where I come from, you do not need a seminar to know people cannot use your lawn as a bathroom.

**MWF:** That makes sense. Your line is clear: your property needs to be treated as property, not as a place someone can contaminate and then debate.

**Darryl:** Yes.

**MWF:** Where are you emotionally right now, from 1 to 10?

**Darryl:** Eight. Angry.

**MWF:** Angry, and also clear. Is there more you need me to understand before we move forward?

**Darryl:** Just that this needs to stop. I do not need him to understand my childhood. I need him to handle it.

**MWF:** You need the behavior to stop, and you need the health and boundary issue taken seriously without making you prove why it matters. Do you feel heard enough to continue?

**Darryl:** Yes. Continue.

## STAGE 2 - PERSPECTIVE STRETCH

### DARRYL'S TRACK

**MWF:** What you did in Stage 1 matters. Now we will try a perspective stretch. You do not have to agree with Shantam or excuse anything. The question is: what might be going on for him?

**Darryl:** I do not know what is going on for him.

**MWF:** That may be true. You may not know his inner world, especially because you are neighbors rather than intimate partners. Let's work only from what you can observe. What have you seen from him?

**Darryl:** He acts confused. Like he does not believe it is happening. Or like he does not want to see it.

**MWF:** So an observational version might be: "When I raised this, you seemed not to take in that this was real for me."

**Darryl:** Sure. He may not know. Or he may not care. I cannot tell.

**MWF:** Good boundary. You do not need to pretend certainty. What could you say that names the impact without claiming you know his motive?

**Darryl:** "Maybe you do not realize what is happening, but from my side there is human waste in my yard and I need you to take it seriously."

**MWF:** That is a real perspective stretch for this kind of relationship. It stays with what you know, leaves room for what you do not know, and still names the impact.

**Darryl:** Fine. That works.

**MWF:** Here is what I would share with Shantam on your behalf: "I do not know exactly what you understand or do not understand about this. From my side, human waste has been showing up in my yard. When I have tried to raise it, it has felt like you do not take in that this is real and serious. I need you to understand the impact without me having to guess your motives." Are you willing to share that?

**Darryl:** Yes.

### SHANTAM'S TRACK

**MWF:** What you did in Stage 1 matters. Now we will try a perspective stretch. Keep the topic frame in view: human waste in Darryl's yard and boundaries around where children use the bathroom. What do you think might be going on for him?

**MWF:** Yes. That is different from analyzing him. Can you put that in your own words to him?

**MWF:** That is focused and grounded. Here is what I would share with Darryl: "I hear that this is about your yard being contaminated and your boundary not being respected. You need me to take it seriously and make sure my child is not using your property this way." Are you willing to share that?
